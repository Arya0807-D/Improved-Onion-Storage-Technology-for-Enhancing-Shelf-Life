# Improved-Onion-Storage-Technology-for-Enhancing-Shelf-Life
IoT-based smart onion storage system integrating MQ135 gas sensing and DHT22 temperature monitoring to detect spoilage and regulate storage conditions. Uses biochar trays and mycelium insulation for passive humidity and heat control. LoRa-enabled SMS alerts ensure real-time monitoring in remote rural areas.

📋 Problem StatementID: 25053 Title: Improved Onion Storage Technology for Enhancing Shelf Life 
Goal: To engineer a sustainable solution to combat catastrophic storage losses in the agriculture sector.
🚀 Proposed Solution
Our system provides a sustainable, high-efficiency storage environment using a combination of natural materials and IoT hardware to reduce storage loss by up to 70%.
Key Features:
Natural Humidity Control: Uses biochar-mycelium trays to manage moisture naturally.
Spoilage Detection: A unique sensor matrix precisely identifies $H_2S$ gas (spoilage volatiles) to pinpoint and remove rotting onions before they infect others.
Passive Cooling: Employs radiative roofs and mycelium walls to cut heat load without using electricity.
Low-Power Connectivity: Integrated LoRa communication sends real-time alerts to a farmer's app, ideal for remote rural areas with limited power.
🛠️ Technical Approach
Hardware & Sensors
MQ135 Gas Sensor: Specifically monitors the air in storage containers to detect $H_2S$ and Ammonia released during spoilage.
DHT22 Sensor: Monitors temperature spikes; it automatically operates fans and air inlets to stabilize the environment.
Mycelium Walls: Act as a thermal barrier and heat insulator made from agro-waste.
Biochar Trays: Absorbs excess humidity and harmful gases that accelerate rotting.Workflow
Passive Mitigation: Radiative roofs reflect sunlight while mycelium walls insulate the unit.
Climate Control: If the DHT22 detects a temperature spike, fans and inlets activate until the temperature stabilizes.
Spoilage Monitoring: MQ135 sensors detect $H_2S$ gas released by rotting onions.Alert System: The sensor matrix identifies the specific area of spoilage and sends the information to the AgriNova Storage Monitor app via LoRa.
📊 Impact & BenefitsEconomic Savings: Projected to save producers up to 30,000 per tonne.Sustainability: Repurposes discarded agro-waste (rice husk, straw) into valuable insulation materials.Energy Efficiency: Optimized for minimal power consumption, allowing sensors to run on small solar panels or extended battery life.
